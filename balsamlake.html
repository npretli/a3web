Located on Highway 48 between the towns of Coboconk and Kirkfield, Balsam Lake is a small provincial park with a variety of hiking trails and activities for visitors, including boating and camping. The park contains a beach, several notable bird species, and a variety of natural landscapes such as open meadows, swamps, and forest.
Hiking

The provincial park contains two short hiking trails which are suitable for hikers of all levels of experience, Lookout Trail and Plantation Trail. Both are interpretive trails that contain signposts with information about the natural and human history of the area.

Find downloadable maps of the trails here (link later), as well as descriptions of each trail below.



Lookout Trail is a 2.6-kilometer trail that takes approximately one and a half hours to walk. Along this trail, you will walk over eskers - ridges of gravel and sediment formed 10,000 years ago by meltwater from the Wisconsin Glacier. Natural landscapes visible along the trail include cedar swamp, deciduous forest, and open meadow. The ‘lookout’ is the highest point on the trail and has a stunning panorama view of the surrounding forest. br>

Plantation Trail is a 4.2-km trail – about a two-hour walk. This trail will take you through landscapes modified by humans, including some of the region’s earliest settlers. You will see old logging sites and farm fields, as well as reforested plantations and a pond teeming with wildlife.
