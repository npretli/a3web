Ken Reid Conservation Area, managed by the Kawartha Conservation area, has 12 day hiking trails as well as shelters and picnic areas for you to enjoy. While relatively small, Ken Reid’s 12 trails total to nearly 11 kilometers. A favorite for bird watchers, the Point Loop (2.7 km) takes you straight out in a peninsula surrounded by Sturgeon Lake. Don’t forget your binoculars!


The Point LoopTakes you near wetlands and mixed forest to a fantastic view over the sturgeon river. An easy hike for all, this short trail will bring you close to some of Ontario's migrating bird species, which often stop here before heading south for the winter.

Plantation Trail is a 4.2-km trail – about a two-hour walk. This trail will take you through landscapes modified by humans, including some of the region’s earliest settlers. You will see old logging sites and farm fields, as well as reforested plantations and a pond teeming with wildlife.
